## POST-PRESENTATION Q&A PREP

### Likely Questions & Data-Driven Answers:

**Q: "How do you balance privacy with age verification?"**  
A: "Privacy-preserving methods exist - zero-knowledge proofs, third-party tokens. Utah's data shows 85% effectiveness with minimal privacy impact. Federal law should mandate these methods, prohibit ID retention, and require annual audits."

**Q: "Won't this hurt small platforms?"**  
A: "Phased rollout: Year 3 for platforms under 1M users. Safe harbor for good-faith compliance. Current patchwork costs $2-5M annually vs $500K-1M for single federal standard."

**Q: "What about state sovereignty?"**  
A: "Tier 2 & 3 preserve state flexibility in 60% of provisions. Federal law is a floor, not ceiling - states can exceed standards. Similar to federal minimum wage or environmental standards."

**Q: "How is this different from failed bills like KOSA?"**  
A: "KOSA is Tier 1 of our framework. We add Tier 2 (state flexibility) and Tier 3 (local control) to address concerns. Plus mandatory sunset and evidence requirements KOSA lacks."

**Q: "What if technology changes?"**  
A: "Technology-neutral language: 'reasonable age verification' not 'government ID.' 5-year sunset forces reauthorization. Annual effectiveness reviews enable iteration."
